What is Phylumbase?

Phylumbase is a way to share and find easily the experimental data generated with WMicrotracker tracking systems ( www.phylumtech.com ). This data mainly concern to scientific or educational experiments using small organisms such as: nematodes, small parasites, fishes and others.

How does it work?

Scientist or Students running experiments may share their experimental data uploading their files using WMicrotracker Software v3.0 and greater. The author can determine if data is going to be public to anyone or just private for his own laboratory.

Can I use the reported experimental data in any way?

Yes, you can. Since public data is shared under creative commons license you can use this information citing the original author contribution (username/labortaory). Please, include the link to PhylumBase webpage.

Do you check the veracity of the information provided by users?

Public information is only for use as a reference of a determined experiment. Phylumbase nor PhylumTech Consortium is not responsible for the veracity of the information published by users. We only determines the veracity that the data is generated with wmicrotracker experiments encript.
