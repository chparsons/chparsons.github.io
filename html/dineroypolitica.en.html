How are our representative democracies working in different parts of the world? How much democratic and representative are they if they depend on funding with highly concentrated origins? By whom and for whom are these democracies working?

This is a prototype for a visualization of the political and financial networks in Argentina. It's based on accountings submitted by political parties and electoral alliances. Here you can see 3 types of agents related:

  • Private Contributors

    • People

    • Companies

  • Money Receivers

    • Political Groups

Data comes from Contributions and Private Donations in CSV format from datospublicos.org which has data from 2007, 2009 and 2011 of campaigns in Argentina. Thanks to dineroypolitica.org for making the data available.


This is a protoype. There's a lot to fix and improve. Currently the network displays elections data in the whole country in all available years. It's not showing data by region nor by year. Data is being filtered to display the 400 bigger nodes for technical reasons.

It's interesenting to note how certain nodes/contributors contribute to different groups working as links between other nodes. These connectors are key to form clusters.

It's also interesting how is the funding structure of the parties, looking at quantities as well as types of funding, i.e. what are the proportions of personal based and company based funds.

If you leave your mouse on a node, the name will appear as a tooltip. You can also click on a node to make a google search with its name. The size of the node represents the total amount of money contributed by the person or company to one or more political organizations.
